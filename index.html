My Story

Full-Stack Web Developer | React/Redux | Node, Express, Massive | Postgresql | RESTful APIs | Trail-Runner

Enthusiasm, growth, adaptability, and flexibility have been the drivers behind the success in my career.

When I was 9 years old I walked downstairs to our basement where my brother was flipping through a book about 5” thick titled HTML. I thought it was awesome that you could create something from nothing and make it look cool on the monitor in front of you. I made some basic “apps” with some headers and some paragraphs - that was the extent and my introduction to coding. In college I started some Code Academy courses, but was full-throttle engaged in my English degree, so those fell by the wayside.

The real motivator that got me seriously interested in coding again was a warm fall day in Cedar Hills, Utah. I was at a client’s house spraying down a giant gardening-bed retaining wall scrubbing down the year-old cured concrete trying to remove the streaks that had layered to the surface. My brain hurt. There was a lack of engagement for it. I’ve spent most of my life engaged in stimulating activities that challenged my brain and body - I need that...a challenge. WIthout a challenge, I tend to get restless.

Enter web development as a serious consideration for my next career. It had everything I needed: problem solving, creativity, complex interactions, and never-ending growth. Critical analysis boosted my syntax-debugging skills. My ability to adapt to new industries and new job requirements increased my ability to learn and integrate into coding faster. Playing a college sport taught me how to work with and contribute effectively to a team. Working with a high-producing sales agent taught me efficiency and thoroughness.

I look forward to the many new and exciting challenges and opportunities software development has to offer me.

DevMountain Job Search App

Job Search Management

GitHub Repo

Built with React, Redux, Node, Postgresql, and Massive. Allows the user to log-in using Auth-0 which enables them to save their information on the user object. They can input companies they are interested in, add contacts which include a time-stamp for the latest action taken with that contact, and add notes related to that contact. Users can search through their companies, view their goals and job-related web-sites, and scroll through a resource drop-down menu that allows you to look at scores of job search resources.
